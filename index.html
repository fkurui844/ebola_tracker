Key facts

  • • No approved vaccine or specific antiviral for Bundibugyo virus
  • • WHO declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) on 17 May 2026
  • • Historical death rate for this virus is 30–50%; current confirmed rate is lower at ~13.4%
  • • Standard GeneXpert test machines cannot detect this strain — special lab testing required (WHO alert)
  • • Two treatment centres were attacked and burned by community members in May 2026 (Rwampara, Mongbwalu)
  • • Oxford University vaccine candidate in animal trials; human trials possible in 2–3 months
  • • US government pledged $462M total: $112M bilateral + $50M for 50 response clinics + $300M humanitarian aid
  • • This is DRC's 17th Ebola outbreak since 1976 and the first Bundibugyo outbreak since 2012

Ebola Treatment Centre (ETC) or isolation ward

A building — hospital or dedicated ward — where confirmed and suspected patients are treated under strict isolation. Run by the Ministry of Health, WHO, or MSF (Doctors Without Borders).

Field isolation unit

A temporary structure (tent or converted space) built quickly near affected communities. Used to isolate patients before they can reach a full hospital ETC — often the first response in remote areas.

What the map tells us

The outbreak is concentrated in a specific corridor

The large dark-red circle over Ituri Province shows that the vast majority of confirmed cases — roughly 71% — are still in the original epicenter. The outbreak began in Mongbwalu, a busy gold-mining town, and spread to Bunia and Rwampara as people sought medical care in the provincial capital.

It has spread south along major travel routes

The orange circles in North and South Kivu show that the virus has followed trade and migration routes southward. Butembo (North Kivu) was also the epicenter of the 2018–2020 Ebola outbreak, meaning communities there have prior experience but also deep distrust of response teams.

Uganda is affected by travel, not local spread

Kampala's blue circle (Uganda) represents cases linked to travel from DRC — not a home-grown chain of transmission. Ituri Province borders Uganda and Bunia is less than 40 km from the border, making cross-border spread an ongoing risk. All 9 confirmed Ugandan cases trace back to DRC contacts.

Treatment facilities are clustered near the epicenter — gaps remain in the south

Most 🏥 markers sit within or near Ituri Province, reflecting the initial response push. The MSF facility under construction in Goma (Munigi) is the primary treatment centre for the growing North Kivu caseload. South Kivu and the border areas still have limited coverage, creating a risk that patients travel long distances — spreading the virus further in the process.

The ⚠️ marker in Kenya shows international political tension

A proposed US quarantine site at Laikipia Air Base in Kenya was blocked by a Kenyan court on 29 May 2026. It is not part of the outbreak zone but represents the international response effort. Kenyan medical unions opposed it, fearing it could introduce the virus into their country. The case is ongoing.
